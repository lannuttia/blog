About Me:

My Professional History:

I'm a software engineer working remotely out of the greater Fayetteville Arkansas area. I graduated from the University of Arkansas in December of 2016 with my Bachelors of Science in Electrical Engineering. Right out of college, I worked at L3Harris Technologies in Greenville Texas. While there, I discovered my love of Software. I did everything from DSP algorithm development, to web service development, to front-end web development, and eventually even CI/CD pipeline development. After several years of working there, I found a job working a J.B. Hunt Transportation. While at J.B. Hunt, I worked on Spring Boot and Quarkus applications for the Carrier 360 platform. Currently I work at Red Hat as a software engineer and spend most of my time maintaining the existing exam scheduler front-end and back-end as well as migrating from the existing legacy Spring back-end and Angular front-end to a suite of Python microservices and a React front-end.

My Hobbies:

I love spending time with my wife and son, playing folk and bluegrass music on my guitar, and playing various games like Factorio and Kerbal Space Program. Despite my love of gaming, I do not own a Windows or Mac computer.
